The Harvest

Our Royal Saffron is harvested during the short autumn blooming season in La Mancha, Spain. Each day begins before dawn, when the delicate Crocus sativus flowers open to reveal their precious crimson stigmas.

Expert harvesters work with traditional wicker baskets, carefully plucking each flower by hand. This meticulous process must be completed within a few hours each morning, as the flowers wilt quickly in the sunlight.

After harvesting, the stigmas are gently separated from the flowers and dried over low heat to preserve their essential oils, vibrant color, and complex aroma. It takes approximately 75,000 flowers to produce just one pound of our premium saffron.

Details

Ingredients & Purity

100% pure Crocus sativus stigmas, hand-selected from Grade A Spanish saffron. No additives, fillers, or color enhancers. Each batch is laboratory-tested for purity and potency, with crocin, picrocrocin, and safranal levels exceeding ISO 3632 Category I standards.

Our saffron contains no artificial preservatives and is packed in UV-protected glass to maintain freshness and prevent degradation from light exposure.

Origin & Terroir

Sourced exclusively from the La Mancha region of Spain, where the unique combination of clay-limestone soils, continental climate, and traditional farming methods creates saffron of exceptional quality.

Each package is traceable to its specific field and harvest date. Our partner farms employ sustainable, biodynamic practices that have been passed down through generations since the 15th century.

Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) certification guarantees authentic La Mancha saffron, recognized worldwide for its superior characteristics.

Pairing Suggestions

Rice Dishes: Perfect for authentic Spanish paella, Persian tahdig, or Italian risotto. Infuse in warm broth before adding to rice.

Seafood: Complements bouillabaisse, Moroccan fish tagines, and lobster bisque. Steep threads in liquid for 15-20 minutes before use.

Baking: Adds golden color and distinctive flavor to breads, buns, and traditional Swedish saffron buns (lussekatter).

Desserts: Elevates crme brle, ice cream, and Middle Eastern milk puddings (muhalabia). Pair with cardamom and rose water.

Beverages: Creates exquisite golden milk, saffron-infused teas, and craft cocktails. A few threads steeped in warm milk release full color and aroma.
